The Sri Lankan wildlife Introduction



Sinharaja Rain Forest

Known as the last viable area of primary tropical rainforest in Sri Lanka, Sinharaja is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. It's a haven for endemic species, including the elusive purple-faced langur and the Sri Lankan leopard.



Horton Plains National Park

Located in the central highlands, Horton Plains is a unique ecosystem with montane grasslands and cloud forests. The park is famous for World's End, a sheer precipice that offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.



Minneriya National Park

Renowned for the Gathering of Elephants, where hundreds of elephants congregate around the Minneriya Tank during the dry season. It's a spectacular sight and one of the best places to witness large herds of these majestic creatures.



Udawalawe National Park

A haven for elephants, Udawalawe is known for its large population of these gentle giants. The park also hosts various bird species and other wildlife. Safari tours offer a close-up experience with the animals in their natural habitat.



Wasgamuwa National Park

Situated in the central province, Wasgamuwa is known for its scenic landscapes and diverse wildlife, including elephants, sloth bears, and leopards. The park is less frequented by tourists, providing a more serene safari experience.
